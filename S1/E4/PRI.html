Moment Of Inertia Of FlyWheel

Aim Principle Equipments Required Procedure Recording Data Precautions Sources of Error Theory Additional Resources
  1. A flywheel converts potential energy of mass into rotational kinetic energy. From this rotational kinetic energy, the moment of inertia of the flywheel is calculated.
  2. The mass, m, is raised to a height h above ground, wound n times. After the mass is released, the flywheel rotates N times with angular velocity ω
$$ \omega = 2 \pi \frac{N}{T} \\ I = \frac{Nm}{N+n} \left[\frac{2gh}{\omega^2} - r^2 \right] $$
